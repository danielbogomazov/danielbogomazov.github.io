Projects

mass-Immunizations

While working coop at the Ontario Ministry of Health and Long-term Care, I worked on an iPad application called mass-Immunizations. It was an application designed to be used by doctors and nurses for logging information on immunizations administered to their patients through the use of modern technologies. It was designed with offline capabilities in mind, leaving only the uploading and downloading of new information to be relient on a wifi connection. Its use comes in the encapsulation of immunization steps into a mobile device and for off-site immunizations such as a school immunization event. It was also designed for the situation of a pandemic event where a mass immunization process would be necessary.

mass-Immunizations was built using Swift 3 and 4. Through tools like Jenkins, Jira, SVN, and GitHub, I was able to work with my team to accomplish my tasks for update releases of the application. I used the CocoaPods dependency manager to work with 3rd party libraries to improve the quality and ease of use of the application.



PocketMoney

PocketMoney is an iOS application I have been working on that would allow users to track their spending habits. It is built using all of the knowledge that I have gathererd throughout my time at college and during my coop work term at the Ministry of Health and Long-term Care.

It uses CoreData to store the model of the user's spendature information, including specific goals and items bought. It is still in production and will be released on the Apple AppStore upon completion. It is an open source project that you can find on my GitHub (linked in the footer).



Workforce++

Workforce++ is a multi-platform application that I designed with 3 other group members. It is an application designed to make shift management easier and to create a messaging service for employees of a business using the product. Managers would be able to create and manage shifts through a web app. Employees are given accounts to sign into the iOS/Android versions which holds all of their schedule information, allows them to request time off, trade shifts, and email other employees. They would also be able to sign in and out for their shifts by scanning their phone with a Raspberry Pi NFC reader.

My role in the group was primarily as the native iOS application developer. My application would be able to communicate with other iOS and Android devices through the server. While in the background, the application would be able to recieve push notifications to notify the user of any shift updates or messages. We used GitHub for centralization and for version control.
